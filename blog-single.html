#2 Discover Iron-Rich Fruits: Boost Your Health at Work.

What is Iron?

Iron is a vital mineral that plays a crucial role in our overall health. It’s a key component of haemoglobin, a protein in our red blood cells that transports oxygen from our lungs to the rest of our bodies. Iron is also crucial in various metabolic processes and supports our immune system. Iron deficiency can lead to anaemia, which can cause fatigue, weakness, and impaired cognitive function. Ensuring an adequate intake of iron and other vitamins and nutrients is essential for maintaining good workplace health.

10 Tasty Iron-Rich Fruits

Incorporating these iron-rich fruits into your diet can be an enjoyable way to increase your iron intake, especially if you have dietary preferences or restrictions that limit your consumption of iron-rich meats or other animal-based sources. Whether you enjoy them as snacks, add them to your meals, or use them in various recipes, these fruits can contribute to your overall health and wellbeing whilst at work.
Surprisingly, watermelon contains a moderate amount of iron, offering around 0.2 mg per 100 grams. It's a hydrating and refreshing fruit, particularly enjoyable during the warmer months.
Pomegranates offer about 0.3 mg of iron per 100 grams. While they may not be as iron-rich as some other fruits, they make up for it with a wealth of antioxidants, vitamins, and unique flavour.
Figs, whether fresh or dried, are a great source of dietary iron. Providing approximately 0.4 mg per 100 grams. They are also high in dietary fibre, calcium, and potassium, as well as providing a unique sweet flavour.
Guava is a tropical fruit packed with iron (around 0.4 mg per 100 grams), vitamin C, dietary fibre, and various antioxidants. It's known for its unique sweet-tart flavour and is a nutritious addition to your fruit salad.
Dates are not only naturally sweet but also rich in iron, providing roughly 0.9 mg of iron per 100 grams. They are also high in natural sugars, making them a quick energy booster. Dates are in energy bars and snacks, making them an ideal choice for a quick iron boost.
Prunes, or dried plums, are exceptionally high in this special mineral, providing about 0.9 mg of iron per 100 grams. They're also packed with dietary fibre, vitamin K, and antioxidants. Prunes are known for their natural laxative properties, making them beneficial for digestive health.
Blackcurrants, tiny berries positively rich in iron, offer roughly 1.8 mg per 100 grams. They are also one of the best natural sources of vitamin C, which enhances our ability to absorb iron.
Raisins, which are dried grapes, are an incredibly accessible source of iron, containing approximately 1.9 mg per 100 grams. They are convenient for snacking and can be added to cereal, yoghurt, or baked goods.
Mulberries are a lesser-known fruit, similar in look to blackberries, and are incredibly nutritious. They’re bursting with approximately 2.1 mg of iron per 100 grams, and are additionally a good source of vitamin C and vitamin K.
Dried apricots are rich in iron, offering a whopping average of 2.7 mg of iron per 100 grams. They're also a good source of dietary fibre, vitamin A, and vitamin C.
